Las meninas

“Las Meninas,” a take on Velázquez’s masterwork. I would like to have made the background darker to emphasize the lighting like Velázquez did, where one person in the foreground might be simultaneously on even ground with the rest and totally dominating the scene. I gave the pen/charcoal drawing away before I could, but am still proud of the likenesses and allusion to art history.

Captain

Another soccer player drawing, in this I focused on capturing intensity to highlight the leadership role that this player is known for. I contrasted the warrior-esque expression and lighting of the face with more simple marking for the background and shirt as a nod to the player’s sole and simple value of loyalty to one team, and to his humble beginnings—in the piece, the glamour of the lights in the background is almost an afterthought, as it was for this player.

Miguelito at night

El Miguelete,” Valencia: the lighting is cyclic. During daytime the sky lights the church tower; at night the streets do. This cathedral reflects both its own religious history and the city over which it towers.
